What you need to know

Covering more than 40 million acres, grass is the largest irrigated crop in the United States. It is a popular fixture in yards, requiring 3 trillion gallons of water, 70 million pounds of pesticides, 200 million gallons of gas each year to maintain. While planting this common mono crop in your front yard might seem harmless, it contributes to algal blooms, drinking water contamination and destroys biodiversity. By blanketing your yard in a grass lawn alternative, you can improve the health of your ecosystem and generate a positive environmental impact.
